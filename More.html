What will happen if no measures are going to be taken.
Losing some of the species - Among the greatest researches that were done by scientists were able to conclude that there are many species that are at risk of extinction. This is all because of pollution caused by human beings. Some of them have already vanished and there are high possibilities that there will be an increase in the next 10 years to come.

Increase in the number of deaths both to animals and human beings - From the statistics and facts above, we can clearly say that the increase in pollution has influenced the increase of deaths of people annually in the global database. This is not only in people but also in other living organisms, animals and plants. All because of the negative impact of human beings on the face of the earth.


Negative impacts to the future generations - Many children about 7 million premature deaths caused by pollution whereby expectant mothers get to inhale toxic air. This means that there are higher chances of the population decreasing into the future generations. Moreover there are high chances that these children surviving in these conditions suffer from mental issues in their lifetime due to the polluted air that they breathe while not born.

Increase in poverty for the next generation due to lack of environmental sustainability - Pollution has been noticed to be among the main factors that affect climate change. This has led to greater increase in terms of weather patterns , increase in extreme drought in the southern hemisphere , More than enough rainfall and temperatures experienced in the northern hemisphere. These are among the main disasters that are influenced by the actions of human beings hence ending up affecting them causing poverty and even deaths.

Cardiovascular diseases - Many of the heart diseases are caused by pollution. They are experienced by many people after having breath in polluted or air that is highly contaminated by poisonous particles.

Particle Pollution and Ground level-ozone pollution - Research reveals that exposure to particle pollution, also known as particulate matter (PM5) can cause cardiovascular problems and can lead to premature death. And ground level-ozone pollution increases the risk of lung diseases. Hence, both combined can result in major environmental and health hazards.

Destroying the ozone layer leading to increased levels of heat waves and increasing global warming in the atmosphere.
